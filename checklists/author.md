# Author checklist

> **DRAFT — for the editorial board to revise.** Placeholder wording. Nothing
> here is in force yet.

Work through this **before** opening a submission issue. The submission form
asks you to confirm it.

This exists to save you time, not to gate you. Every item is something that
would otherwise come back as a revision request weeks later.

---

## The work

- [ ] It concerns **GeoGebra in mathematics education**, K–16
- [ ] It is **original** and not published elsewhere
- [ ] It is **not under review** at another journal
- [ ] All named authors agree to the submission and to publication
- [ ] Everyone who qualifies as an author is listed, and nobody who doesn't

## The manuscript

- [ ] Prepared from the
      [article template](https://github.com/NorthAmericanGeoGebraJournal/article-template)
- [ ] Has a title, abstract, and keywords
- [ ] References are complete, and prior work is credited
- [ ] Figures are legible at print size and captioned
- [ ] Mathematics is correct, and you have checked it

## Reproducibility

**Could a reader recreate what you describe?** They need at least one of:

- [ ] a link to a working GeoGebra resource, **or**
- [ ] the construction commands, **or**
- [ ] figures and a described procedure a reader can follow

- [ ] At least one of the above is genuinely present

> A paper with no applet is entirely acceptable. A paper a reader cannot follow
> is not.

## GeoGebra resource *(if you have one)*

Encouraged, **not required**. NAGJ does not host applets — you keep yours, and we
record its permanent URL.

- [ ] It is publicly accessible without a login
- [ ] The URL is permanent — normally `geogebra.org/m/…`
- [ ] It loads and does what the paper says
- [ ] It survives ordinary use: labelled, sensible defaults, doesn't break when
      dragged
- [ ] You will keep it available at that URL after publication

## Rights and disclosure

- [ ] You hold the rights to everything included — text, figures, screenshots,
      data
- [ ] Third-party material is used with permission and credited
- [ ] You agree to publish under **CC BY 4.0** *(draft — board to confirm; see below)*
- [ ] Conflicts of interest are declared (see
      [the policy](../policies/conflicts-of-interest.md))
- [ ] Funding sources are named in the manuscript
- [ ] Work involving students or classroom data had whatever approval your
      institution requires

> **On the licence** *(board to confirm)*: CC BY 4.0 lets anyone reuse your
> work with credit — which is the point of publishing here, since our readers
> are teachers who adapt things. Articles from the journal's OJS era carry
> CC BY-NC-SA 4.0; that cannot be changed retroactively, so the archive is
> mixed. Each article records its own terms.

## Open review — read this one properly

NAGJ reviews are **public and signed**. If you submit:

- [ ] The review conversation happens in a **public GitHub issue** anyone can read
- [ ] Reviewers are **named**, and so are you
- [ ] The thread is **permanent and citable**, and stays public whether the paper
      is accepted or rejected
- [ ] You understand a **rejection remains visible**

- [ ] You accept this

> This is the deal open review makes: reviewers are accountable for what they
> say, authors can see who said it, and the record can be cited. It also means a
> rejection is public. If that isn't for you, NAGJ isn't the right venue, and
> that's a reasonable decision to make now rather than later.

## Practical

- [ ] Your manuscript is in a **public repository**, or you can share it another
      way — say so in the submission issue
- [ ] You can respond to reviewers within a reasonable time
- [ ] You have a GitHub account, and it's the one you'll use

---

## What happens next

1. You open a **submission issue**.
2. An editor checks scope and conflicts — usually within *(board to set a target)*.
3. If it fits, the editor assigns reviewers and posts their checklists in the
   issue.
4. Reviewers work in the open. **You can respond as they go** — this is a
   conversation, not a verdict delivered at the end.
5. You revise; the editor decides.
6. On acceptance, the article is published with a permanent link back to this
   review record.

There are **no submission fees and no publication fees**, at any stage.
