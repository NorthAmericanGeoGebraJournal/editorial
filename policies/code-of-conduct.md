## Scope

This applies to everyone taking part in NAGJ — authors, reviewers, editors, and
anyone commenting on an issue in this repository.

## The standard

Review is public here. That raises the stakes: every word you write is
permanent, attributed, and readable by the author's colleagues and students.

- **Critique the work, never the person.** "This proof is wrong" is a review.
  "You don't understand geometry" is not.
- **Assume competence.** The author is a colleague who has spent longer on this
  than you have.
- **Be specific.** "Unclear" helps nobody. Say which sentence, and why.
- **Say what works.** Public praise is permanent too, and it costs you nothing.
- **Disagree openly and plainly.** Open review exists so disagreement is visible.
  Sharp is fine; contemptuous is not.
- **Take corrections in public.** Reviewers are wrong sometimes. Say so and move
  on.

## Not acceptable

Harassment, demeaning or discriminatory comments, personal attacks, publishing
private information, sustained disruption, or using a review to settle something
unrelated to the manuscript.
