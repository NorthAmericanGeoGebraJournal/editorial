## Authorship

Authors are those who made a substantive intellectual contribution. Everyone who
qualifies is listed; nobody who does not. Disputes are settled before submission,
not during review.

## Originality

Submissions must be original, unpublished, and not under review elsewhere.
Building on your own earlier work is fine — cite it. Reusing your own text
without citation is not.

## Plagiarism

Text, figures, constructions, or ideas taken from others must be credited. This
includes GeoGebra constructions: if you adapted someone's applet, say so and link
it.

## Data and classroom work

Work involving students or classroom data must have whatever approval your
institution requires, and must not identify students without consent.

## Corrections after publication

Open a **correction issue** in this repository.

- **Correction** — a factual error that does not undermine the conclusions. The
  article is updated and the correction noted.
- **Retraction** — findings are unreliable, or there was misconduct. The article
  stays online, marked retracted, with the reason. *(Board to confirm.)*

Published articles are **not silently edited**. The record is the record — that
is what makes it worth citing.

## AI

*(Board to decide. A first draft:)*

- **Authors** must disclose material use of generative AI in preparing a
  manuscript. AI cannot be an author.
- **Reviewers** must not paste an unpublished manuscript into a third-party AI
  service — that is a confidentiality breach even though the review is public,
  because the manuscript may not be.
- **The journal** does not use AI to evaluate submissions, recommend decisions,
  or generate reviews. Automation here handles process only.
